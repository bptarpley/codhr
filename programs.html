Programs

We are proud to offer the following programs to TAMU affiliates. Questions? Contact us at codhr@tamu.edu.

Project Support and Development

The Libraries have generously provided the technical infrastructure we need to continue hosting CoDHR’s broad portfolio of faculty and graduate student projects. We’ve been meeting with collaborators from these projects to further develop and support them. If you’re a TAMU affiliated faculty or graduate student and have an idea for a project or would like help furthering an existing one, please don’t hesitate to reach out at codhr@tamu.edu

Digital Humanities Consultation

CoDHR Office Hours are back! We camp out in one of the glass consultation booths at the front of the Evans Library on Tuesdays from 3-5PM and would love to meet with you to discuss all things DH. Not able to make it to the Libraries during that time slot? No problem—we’re also on Zoom at https://tamu.zoom.us/my/dh.support. Either way, drop in and we’ll support you on a first come, first served basis.

Digital Humanities Speaker Series

As part of our remit to create engagement and learning opportunities for the DH community on campus we will continue to bring in guest speakers from the broader discipline. This semester we’re excited to announce two events. We’ve partnered with Dr. Shawna Ross in English to help sponsor Alex Gil, who teaches DH courses and runs project-based learning and research initiatives at Yale University. Alex will be coming on Friday, March 20th. We’re also bringing in Scott Kleinman, Director of the CSUN Center for Digital Humanities, who will be speaking on Thursday, April 9th. We’ll be providing more details about these events in the coming weeks.
